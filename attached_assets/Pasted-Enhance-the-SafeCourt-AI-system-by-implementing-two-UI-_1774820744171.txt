Enhance the **SafeCourt AI** system by implementing two UI/UX features:

A) Simplify Toggle (Final Verdict ONLY)
B) Hover-based Legal Term Glossary

Tech Stack:

* React + TypeScript (Vite)
* Tailwind CSS

---

# 🎯 GOAL

Improve usability while maintaining legal authenticity:

* Keep debate in professional legal language
* Allow users to simplify ONLY the final verdict
* Help users understand legal terms via hover tooltips

---

# 🔴 FEATURE A: SIMPLIFY TOGGLE (FINAL VERDICT ONLY)

## Functional Requirements

1. Add a toggle switch labeled:
   **"Simplify Verdict"**

2. Scope:

   * Apply ONLY to the **Final Verdict**
   * DO NOT modify Defense or Prosecution arguments

---

## Behavior

### When Toggle = OFF (default):

* Show original verdict (legal language)

### When Toggle = ON:

* Replace verdict with simplified version
* Preserve exact meaning (no distortion)

---

## Simplification Rules

* Convert legal language into plain English
* Keep meaning identical
* Use short, clear sentences
* Avoid jargon and complex phrasing

Examples:

* "prima facie case" → "enough initial evidence"
* "laches" → "unreasonable delay"
* "injunctive relief" → "court order to stop an action"

---

## UI Behavior

* When ON:

  * Show simplified verdict as primary text
  * Optionally show original verdict below in faded/light text

* Add smooth transition (fade or switch)

* Keep layout consistent (no shifting)

---

## Implementation Suggestion

State:

* `isSimplified: boolean`

Logic:

* Store original verdict
* Generate simplified version (via API or transformation)
* Toggle between both views

---

# 🔵 FEATURE B: HOVER-BASED LEGAL GLOSSARY

## Functional Requirements

1. Detect and highlight legal terms in:

   * Debate (Defense + Prosecution)
   * Final Verdict

---

## Terms to Highlight (examples)

* prima facie
* estoppel
* laches
* injunction
* burden of proof
* constructive notice

DO NOT highlight:

* common words like court, judge, plaintiff, defendant

---

## Tooltip Behavior

On hover (desktop) or long press (mobile):

Format: <term>
→ <short meaning>

Examples:

* Prima Facie → Enough evidence at first glance
* Laches → Delay that weakens a legal claim
* Estoppel → Prevented from denying a fact

---

## Tooltip Rules

* Max 8–12 words
* Single line only
* No paragraphs
* No examples
* Instant appear/disappear
* No delay or animation lag

---

## UI Design

* Highlight terms subtly (underline or dotted underline)
* Tooltip:

  * Small floating box
  * Dark or neutral background
  * Rounded corners
  * Clean typography

---

## Implementation Suggestion

* Create a dictionary/map:
  `{ term: definition }`

* Wrap detected terms in:
  `<span>` with hover behavior

* Build reusable component:
  `GlossaryTooltip.tsx`

---
